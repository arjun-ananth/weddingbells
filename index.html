Dear friends and family,

We are excited to gather a handful of the people we love most in one place. We envisioned this big day as an intimate celebration with those who matter most to us. And that's you.

Our story is not the typical fairy tale. We did not grow up in the same place or go to the same school. We come from different worlds — Arjun from Bangalore, India, and Lara from Manila, Philippines — connected by chance and, eventually, a whole lot of heart.

It’s not without its quirks. Arjun is vegetarian and Lara is not. Our playlists don’t match, and our cultures bring two different, colorful worlds. Our worlds are different, but together, we’ve created a new one—ours.

This celebration is exactly that — our version of balance and collision. A black-tie evening where we get to dress up and toast under the chandeliers. A Hindu ritual where tradition meets vibrance in the heart of Little India, Singapore.

We know that some of you are traveling to be here, and we do not take that lightly. Our deepest wish is that while you’re with us, you don’t feel like guests at a wedding, but rather, cherished friends and family at a home away from home.

So come as you are. Bring your joy, your stories, and your appetite (trust us, there will be food for every persuasion — veg and non-veg alike). Together, let’s enjoy an unforgettable few days.

With love, laughter, and a little bit of kare-kare meets sambar,

Lara & Arjun

Our Story

How we met

In June 2022, things started to unravel — or maybe fall into place. Lara’s previous boss called with some news: a job opportunity had opened in Singapore, if she wanted it. Lara jumped at the chance for a new adventure, not knowing that her life was about to change forever.

That weekend turned out to be unexpected in every way. A chance encounter: Lara met Arjun — two people coincidentally working in banking technology — and somehow, they just clicked.

Then, almost as if life had been holding its breath, Lara got the job offer from that very interview — a whole year later. Who would have thought?

Now, three years later, Lara and Arjun have been building their life and memories together in Singapore. The rest, as they say, is history.

What we love

About each other

Q: What do you love about Arjun?
Lara: He’s steady and grounded, someone I can always rely on. He leads with quiet confidence, never needing to put on a show. Behind his discipline is a deep kindness and a gentleness that makes me feel safe, cherished, and loved every single day.

Q: What do you love about Lara?
Arjun: She's the kind of person who carries her heart on her sleeve—genuine to the core, guided by empathy, and never by malice. There’s a quiet strength in her kindness, and a boundless joy in her laughter.

Q&A

Good to know

When should I arrive and leave?

Most guests are flying in from Saturday, Nov 29, to Thursday, Dec 4. Celebration Day 1 is on Monday. Arriving on the weekend gives you time to settle in, relax, and enjoy the city before the festivities begin!

Where should I stay?

Most of the bride’s and groom’s families will be staying around Marina Bay Sands (MBS) and the Orchard area. Singapore is a compact city, so anywhere you choose will likely be within a 15-minute Grab ride to both the wedding events. Pick a place that feels comfortable and convenient for you—it’s all about enjoying your stay!

Ideas for free days?
  • Christmas Wonderland at Gardens by the Bay
  • Orchard Road Christmas Light-Up & Holiday Bazaar
  • Studio Ghibli Exhibition
  • Sentosa Island activities
  • Mandai Wildlife Reserve, Harry Potter: Visions of Magic, and the new Singapore Oceanarium
Can I take photos?

Yes. Please tag us on Facebook or Instagram so we can see all the moments you capture — and maybe even repost a few.

Transportation tips

MRT, bus, Grab, and taxis are convenient. Make sure to carry a credit card per person for MRT top-ups if needed.

No entourage. Why?

By design. Everyone is handpicked, close to our hearts, making this day truly intimate.

How will the solemnization ceremony flow?

A solemnizer will facilitate the ceremony: welcome, bridal march, exchange of vows and rings, and signing of the marriage certificate.
